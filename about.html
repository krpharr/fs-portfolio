Born on Titan, the second moon of Saturn, and unwittingly brought to earth by an accident with a wormhole, I was adopted and raised by a pack of wolves in the mountains of southwest Virginia. After a chance encounter with interstellar jazz musicians in my early teens, I gleaned my means of escape and prospects of living my life as a musician.

From 1988 to 1992, I attended the Jazz Studies program at Virginia Commonwealth Univerisity and did well in music but failed miserably at college because wolves know nothing of higher education. Through my professional experience I was hired by Virginia Commonwealth Univerisity in 2005 to teach upright and electric jazz bass, jazz improvisation, and small jazz ensembles. In addition, I was hired in 2011 to teach jazz bass at the University of Richmond.

Another lifelong passion of mine has been tinkering with computer languages. I played with Basic in my early teens on a Commodore Vic 20 and picked back up in my twenties with C and C++ and then again in my early forties with Python. After 30 years of working self-employed as a professional musician including the last 14 years part-time adjunct faculty, I am working on strengthening my skills in computer languages and gaining the education and experience needed in the vast industry to diversify my employment opportunities.
